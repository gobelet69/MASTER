Whats objective law,Objective law is the set of rules governing relations between members of a same society;
Whats subjective law,Subjective rights are specific prerogatives or rights held by individuals;
3 principle of the separation law,The system is based on 1. Legislative power (creating rules of conduct), 2. Executive power (executing and administering rules), and 3. Judicial power (applying rules to specific cases);
3 traditional power,The powers are 1. Legislative power (The King, Federal Government, and Parliament), 2. Executive power (The King and Federal Government), and 3. Judicial power (Courts and tribunals);
Hierarchy of legal norms,A structured ranking where higher rules prevail over lower ones, moving from supranational treaties down to local regulations;
What is the highest level of the hierarchy,The supranational level, which includes the ECHR and European Union treaties;
What is the highest national norm,The Constitution;
What level follows the Constitution,Federal laws, regional and community decrees, and ordinances;
What level follows federal and regional acts,Royal orders, governmental orders, and ministerial orders;
What is the lowest level of the hierarchy,Provincial and communal regulations and ordinances;
What does the constitutional court check and what rules they use for check,It checks laws, decrees, and ordinances against the Constitution (Title II and distribution of powers rules);
What does the Council of State check and what rules they use for check,It checks decisions of various government levels against the Constitution, laws, general principles, and treaties;
What does the ECHR check and what rules they use for check,The European Court of Human Rights checks national legislation and practices against the European Convention on Human Rights;
What does the ECJ check and what rules they use for check,The European Court of Justice checks the legality of EU acts and Member State compliance using EU Law;
3 subdivision and branches of law,The branches are 1. Public law (state-citizen relations), 2. Private law (individual relations), and 3. IT security law (a cross-disciplinary field involving privacy and telecom law);
Who is the magistrate,The magistrate;
Who is the court clerk,The court clerk;
Who is the lawyer,The lawyer;
Who is the bailiff,The bailiff;
Define the magistrate,A judicial player who can be a sitting judge deciding cases or a prosecutor representing the state;
Define the court clerk,The official responsible for the authenticity of judicial acts and maintaining court files;
Define the lawyer,An independent advisor who defends the client’s subjective rights in the legal system;
Define the bailiff,A public officer responsible for notifying parties of legal actions and enforcing judgments;
What is material competence,Material competence determines jurisdiction based on the object, value, urgency, or quality of the parties in a dispute;
What is territorial jurisdiction,Territorial jurisdiction determines authority based on geographic areas like judicial cantons, districts, or provinces;
Hierarchy of courts,The Belgian judicial hierarchy consists of Lower Courts, Specialized Tribunals, Higher Court, and the Court of Cassation;
Lower Courts,This level contains Civil magistrates and Police tribunals;
Specialized Tribunals,This level contains Entreprise tribunals and Labour tribunals;
Higher Court,This level contains Appeal Courts, Labor Courts, and Assize Courts;
Court of Cassation,The highest level that reviews the legality of last-instance decisions without re-examining the facts;
